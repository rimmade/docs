When you need to bring customer intelligence to a stakeholder meeting, a board review, or a cross-functional sync, Syncly reports give you a presentation-ready snapshot of your dashboard data without any manual formatting. With one click, Syncly compiles your widgets, applies your current filters, and generates a clean report — including an AI-written Smart Brief that summarizes the key takeaways — so you spend your time acting on insights instead of assembling slide decks.

## What Are Syncly Reports?

A Syncly report is a formatted, shareable document generated directly from a dashboard. It captures the state of your dashboard at the moment of generation — all your widgets, filters, and data — and presents them in a polished layout designed for stakeholders who may not have daily access to Syncly. Reports include a **Smart Brief**, an AI-generated executive summary that highlights the most significant trends, anomalies, and signals found in the data.

You can export a report as a static PDF, or share a live link that always reflects the most recently generated version.

## Generate a Report from a Dashboard

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the dashboard you want to report on">
    Navigate to the dashboard in the left sidebar. Make sure any global filters — date range, source, segment — are set the way you want them to appear in the report before you proceed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the Report button">
    Click the **Report** button in the top-right corner of the dashboard. The report configuration panel slides open.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your report options">
    Configure the report before generating it:

    - **Title** — customize the report title, or keep the default (your dashboard name plus the date).
    - **Date range** — confirm or adjust the time window the report should cover.
    - **Smart Brief summary** — this toggle is on by default. Syncly's AI reads your dashboard data and writes a concise executive summary at the top of the report. Leave it enabled unless you have a specific reason to exclude it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Preview the report">
    Click **Preview** to see exactly how the report will look before you share or export it. Scroll through the full layout, check that all widgets are rendering correctly, and review the Smart Brief for accuracy.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export as PDF or share a live link">
    When you're satisfied with the preview, choose your distribution method:

    - **Export PDF** — downloads a static, print-ready PDF to your device.
    - **Copy Live Link** — generates a URL that stakeholders can open in any browser to view the report without needing a Syncly account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Schedule Recurring Reports

Instead of generating reports manually each week, you can set up a recurring schedule so Syncly produces and delivers them automatically.

1. Open the dashboard you want to schedule.
2. Click **Report → Schedule Report**.
3. Choose a frequency: **Weekly** (pick a day and time) or **Monthly** (pick a day of the month and time).
4. Add recipients — enter email addresses or choose a connected Slack channel.
5. Click **Save Schedule**.

Syncly generates the report at the scheduled time and delivers it to every recipient automatically. You can edit or cancel the schedule at any time from **Settings → Scheduled Reports**.

## Share Reports

Once a report is generated, you have three ways to get it to the right people.

| Method | Best For |
|---|---|
| **Email** | Sending to stakeholders outside your Syncly workspace, or anyone who prefers a formal attachment |
| **Slack** | Dropping the report link or PDF directly into a team channel for quick visibility |
| **Download (PDF)** | Attaching to a presentation, uploading to a shared drive, or printing for an in-person meeting |

<Tip>
  Schedule a recurring report every Monday morning at 8:00 am and add your leadership team as recipients. They'll have a fresh, AI-summarized overview of the previous week's customer sentiment and key topics waiting in their inbox before the week kicks off — no requests, no manual prep required.
</Tip>

<Note>
  **Live links vs. static PDFs:** A live link always displays the most recently generated version of the report, so recipients who open it later will see updated content if you've regenerated the report since sharing. A static PDF, on the other hand, is a permanent snapshot — it never changes after export. Use live links for ongoing stakeholder visibility and PDFs when you need an immutable record, such as for quarterly business reviews or compliance purposes.
</Note>
