Knowing what your competitors are doing on social video isn't just useful context — it's a strategic advantage. Syncly Social's Competitive Analysis feature monitors your rivals' video presence across 10+ platforms, analyzes what's driving their engagement, and surfaces the creator partnerships and content strategies that are working for them right now. You stop reacting to competitors and start getting ahead of them.

## What Competitive Analysis Does

Competitive Analysis applies Syncly Social's full AI stack — Audio Intelligence, AI Vision, sentiment analysis, and engagement tracking — to your competitors' public social presence. It answers the questions that are hardest to answer manually: which content formats are actually performing, which creators are amplifying their brand, and how audience sentiment toward their brand is trending over time.

You get a side-by-side view of your brand versus competitors across every metric that matters, updated continuously so your insights stay current.

## Setting Up Competitor Tracking

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Competitive Analysis in Syncly Social">
    Log in at [creator.syncly.app](https://creator.syncly.app) and navigate to the Competitive Analysis section from the main sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add competitors by brand name, handles, and keywords">
    Enter each competitor's brand name and social handles. Add relevant product keywords and campaign hashtags to ensure comprehensive tracking. You can add multiple competitors and group them by category or region.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select platforms to track">
    Choose the platforms where you want to monitor competitor activity. You can track different platforms for different competitors based on where each is most active.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View the competitive dashboard">
    Once tracking is active, the competitive dashboard begins populating with data. Depending on competitor activity and platform data availability, you'll start seeing insights within hours.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Reading the Competitive Dashboard

The competitive dashboard gives you a structured, side-by-side comparison of your brand versus each tracked competitor:

- **Mention volume comparison** — see how your brand's mention volume stacks up against competitors across all platforms and over time. Identify when a competitor's volume spikes and investigate what drove it.
- **Sentiment comparison** — compare sentiment scores across brands to see where you're winning on perception and where competitors are pulling ahead.
- **Top content by engagement** — surface the competitor posts and videos generating the most engagement, so you can analyze what's working for them at a content level.
- **Creator activity** — see which creators are publishing content about each competitor, their reach, and how their audience responded.
- **Platform share** — understand which platforms each competitor is prioritizing and where their share of voice is growing or declining.
- **Theme and topic breakdown** — Conversation Insights breaks down what audiences are talking about in competitor brand discussions, revealing the narratives competitors are winning and losing.

## Competitive Sentiment Comparison

The sentiment comparison view puts your brand's sentiment trend alongside each competitor's on a single chart. This makes it immediately visible when a competitor is experiencing a sentiment dip — which often signals an opportunity to capture their dissatisfied audience with targeted messaging. Equally, if a competitor's sentiment is rising sharply, you can investigate the content or campaign driving that shift and decide how to respond.

<Tip>
  Before briefing your creative team for an upcoming campaign, run a competitive analysis to identify the content formats and themes that are currently outperforming for rivals in your category. Use those findings as a starting point for your brief — not to copy what competitors are doing, but to understand the baseline your campaign needs to beat.
</Tip>

<Note>
  Competitive data is sourced from publicly available social content. Content set to private, restricted, or limited distribution on any platform is not accessible to Syncly Social. Coverage and data depth may vary by platform depending on their public content policies.
</Note>
