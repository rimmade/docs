Some questions can't be answered by a dashboard widget. When you need to understand the *full story* behind a customer trend — why it's happening, which customers are affected, how it has evolved, and what they're actually saying — Insight Explorer is where you go. It's a flexible, open-ended analysis workspace that lets you pull any slice of your feedback data, view it from multiple angles, and save your findings so your whole team benefits from the work. Think of it as a research environment built specifically for customer intelligence.

## What Insight Explorer Is

Insight Explorer gives you a canvas for investigating specific questions about your feedback data. Unlike dashboards, which show you a pre-built view of predefined metrics, Insight Explorer lets you start with a question — "What are enterprise customers saying about our export feature?" or "Has sentiment around onboarding improved since our redesign?" — and build the analysis that answers it.

Every analysis you create in Insight Explorer is interactive and reproducible. You can return to it tomorrow, update the time range, and see fresh results without starting over.

## How to Open Insight Explorer

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="From the nav">
    Click **Insight Explorer** in the left navigation sidebar. This opens a blank analysis workspace where you can start from scratch.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="From the Feedback Hub">
    In the Feedback Hub, select one or more feedback items and click **Explore in Insight Explorer** from the action bar. The workspace opens pre-filtered to show feedback matching the same tags and sources as your selection.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="From a Trending Topic">
    On the Trending Topics page, open any topic's detail panel and click **Investigate in Insight Explorer**. The workspace opens pre-configured with the topic's tag, the current trending time window, and the sources driving the spike — so you can jump straight into investigation without any manual setup.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="From a saved analysis">
    Navigate to **Insight Explorer → My Analyses** to reopen any analysis you've previously saved. You can also access shared analyses your teammates have published to the team library under **Team Analyses**.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Creating an Analysis

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a topic, tag, or keyword">
    Start by defining what you want to investigate. Use the **Topic** field to enter:

    - A **tag** from your taxonomy (e.g., "Onboarding" or "Data Export")
    - A **keyword or phrase** to search across all feedback text (e.g., "too slow" or "can't find")
    - A **topic cluster** generated by Syncly's AI, which groups semantically related feedback even when customers use different words

    You can combine multiple topics with AND / OR logic to refine your scope.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set time range and source filters">
    Use the **Date Range** picker to define the period you want to analyze. Then use the **Sources** filter to include or exclude specific integrations — for example, analyze only support tickets, or only in-app feedback submissions.

    Additional filters available:

    - **Sentiment** — limit the analysis to positive, neutral, or negative feedback
    - **Customer segment** — scope to a specific plan tier, company size, or any custom segment
    - **Language** — analyze feedback in a specific language
  </Step>
  <Step title="View matching feedback, sentiment breakdown, and volume chart">
    Once your filters are set, the analysis workspace populates three panels:

    - **Feedback list** — every matching feedback item, sorted by relevance. Click any item to read the full text in the detail panel on the right.
    - **Sentiment breakdown** — a donut chart showing the distribution of Positive, Neutral, and Negative sentiment across all matching items, with the Net Sentiment Score displayed in the center.
    - **Volume chart** — a bar chart showing feedback volume per day (or per week for longer time ranges). Hover any bar to see the exact count and sentiment breakdown for that period.

    All three panels update in real time as you adjust your filters.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save as a named analysis">
    When you have a configuration worth keeping, click **Save Analysis** in the top-right corner. Enter a name (e.g., "Onboarding – Q2 2024") and an optional description. Saved analyses appear in **My Analyses** and can be shared with your team. The next time you open the analysis, it runs against the current data with the same filter configuration, so results are always fresh.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Comparing Two Topics or Time Periods Side by Side

Comparison is often where the most powerful insights emerge. Insight Explorer's **Compare Mode** lets you place two analyses next to each other in a split-screen layout.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable Compare Mode">
    Click the **Compare** toggle in the top toolbar of any open analysis. The workspace splits into two panes, with your current analysis on the left.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the right pane">
    The right pane starts as a copy of your current analysis. Change the topic, adjust the time range, or swap the source filter to create the comparison you want. For example:

    - **Topic comparison**: Left pane = "Onboarding", Right pane = "Data Export" — see which topic generates more negative sentiment
    - **Time period comparison**: Left pane = January–March, Right pane = April–June — measure whether improvements have had an effect
    - **Segment comparison**: Left pane = Free plan customers, Right pane = Enterprise customers — understand how different user groups experience the same feature
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the comparison summary">
    Below the two panes, Syncly generates a **Comparison Summary** — a plain-language description of the key differences between the two analyses, including volume differences, sentiment delta, and the top feedback themes unique to each side.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Exporting Analysis Results

Share your findings with stakeholders who don't work in Syncly, or bring data into external tools for further processing.

<Accordion title="Export to CSV">
  Click **Export → CSV** to download all matching feedback items with their metadata — text, source, date, tags, sentiment score, and customer segment. The CSV respects your current filters, so you export exactly what you see in the analysis.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Export to PDF">
  Click **Export → PDF** to generate a formatted report containing the sentiment breakdown chart, the volume chart, the comparison summary (if in Compare Mode), and a sample of the top feedback items. PDF exports are ready to drop into a slide deck or share via email.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Share a live link">
  Click **Share** to generate a shareable link to the saved analysis. Recipients with a Syncly account can open the link and view a live, read-only version of the analysis. Recipients without an account see a static snapshot. You can set the link to expire after a defined period from the Share settings panel.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Push to integrations">
  If you have a Notion, Confluence, or Jira integration connected, you can push analysis results directly to those tools. Click **Export → Send to…** and select your destination. Syncly formats the content appropriately for each platform — a Notion page, a Confluence article, or a Jira issue description.
</Accordion>

<Tip>
  Save your most frequently used analyses as **bookmarks** by clicking the bookmark icon next to any saved analysis in the My Analyses list. Bookmarked analyses appear at the top of the Insight Explorer home screen and in the Quick Access section of the left sidebar. If you run the same analysis every week — say, "Negative feedback from the past 7 days, by tag" — bookmarking it turns a multi-step setup into a single click.
</Tip>
